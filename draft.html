Intentional Self-harm Deaths (Suicide) in Australia

This visualisation shows the distribution of deaths caused by intentional self harm in Australia, categorised by state and territory, and by age groups.

Suicide by State and Territory

The map highlights the stark contrast between regions, with the Northern Territory exhibiting the highest rate at 19.7 per 100,000 people, underscoring a concerning issue. In contrast, Victoria's 10.5 rate sheds light on factors and emphasizes targeted interventions and support.

Age-standardised Suicide Rates

In order to measure changes over time, age-standardised suicide rates are presented in the graph on the left. Australians aged between 35 to 54, and 85 and above were more likely to die by suicide than those in other age cohorts. The highest death rate of 21.3 was achieved by the 45 to 54 year-old age group at 2015.
