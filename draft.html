Intentional Self-harm Deaths
(Suicide) in Australia

The profound impact of each life lost to suicide on families, friends, workplaces, and the broader community is significant. Recent data from the Australian Bureau of Statistics (ABS) reveals that approximately 9 lives are lost to suicide daily.

It's estimated that over 10 million Australian adults are connected to a suicide victim, and 1 in 2 young people are impacted by suicide before age 25, underscoring the pressing need for heightened awareness and support for this critical issue.

Suicide by State and Territory

Analyzing data from 2012 to 2022, a consistent pattern emerges. The Northern Territory consistently records the highest suicide rate, emphasizing the ongoing need for substantial intervention and support. Conversely, New South Wales and Victoria consistently exhibit the lowest rates, suggesting potential successful strategies.

Age-standardised Suicide Rates

In order to measure changes over time, age-standardised suicide rates are presented in the graph on the left. Australians aged between 35 to 54, and 85 and above were more likely to die by suicide than those in other age cohorts. The highest death rate of 21.3 was achieved by the 45 to 54 year-old age group at 2015. The age group 0-14 years old was ignored because maximum rate is less than 1.

Risk Factors

Individuals can have multiple concurrent risk factors, leading to the percentages for specific risk factors overlapping. This results in cumulative percentages for risk factors in a year exceeding 100%.

Depressive episode ranks as the most prevalent risk factor in cases of suicide, significantly contributing to the overall proportion across the years.

Finding Help and Support

24-hour telephone assistance is available through:

  • Lifeline - 13 11 14
  • Suicide Call Back Service - 1300 659 467
  • MensLine Australia - 1300 789 978
  • Kids Helpline - 1800 551 800
  • StandBy - Support After Suicide - 1300 727 247
  • National Alcohol and Other Drugs Hotline - 1800 250 015

If you, or someone you know is in immediate danger, please call 000, visit your nearest hospital or call any of the above services.
